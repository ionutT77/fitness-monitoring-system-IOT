What Actually Drives the Heart Rate Metrics
Metrics like avg_hr (average heart rate), max_hr (maximum heart rate), hr_spikes, and pct_time_low (percentage of time in a low heart rate zone) are governed by your cardiovascular system and autonomic nervous system, not your body type.

Max HR: This is primarily determined by genetics and age. It does not fluctuate based on body composition or fitness level.

Average HR and HR Spikes: These are dictated by your cardiovascular fitness (specifically, stroke volume—how much blood your heart pumps per beat) and your effort level. A highly trained endurance athlete will have a lower avg_hr and fewer hr_spikes during a set workload than an untrained person, regardless of whether they look like an "ectomorph" or an "endomorph."

Recovery (pct_time_low): How quickly your heart rate drops back into a low zone after a spike is a direct measure of aerobic conditioning and parasympathetic nervous system function.

What Actually Drives the Muscular Metrics
Metrics like avg_emg (muscle activation), emg_fatigue (neuromuscular fatigue), and total_reps are governed by your central nervous system and muscle fiber composition.

Muscle Fiber Typing: Humans have a mix of Type I (slow-twitch, fatigue-resistant) and Type II (fast-twitch, powerful but fatigue quickly) muscle fibers. Someone with a high percentage of Type II fibers will generate higher initial avg_emg readings and higher power output, but their emg_fatigue will rise rapidly, reducing their total_reps in a high-intensity set.

Neuromuscular Efficiency: A trained weightlifter's brain is better at recruiting motor units simultaneously. This leads to higher avg_emg signals compared to a beginner, whose nervous system cannot yet activate as much of the muscle at once.

Leverages and Biomechanics: This is the one area where physical shape matters, but in terms of bone length, not "somatotype." Someone with long arms has to move the weight further during a bench press, doing more mechanical work per rep, which can induce earlier emg_fatigue and lower total_reps compared to someone with shorter arms lifting the same weight.
